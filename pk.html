POCKET KITCHEN | MASTER'S PROJECT

My Master's project applying machine vision to help reduce household food waste! I was awarded an MEng degree in Design Engineering with First Class honours, achieving 81% in this project overall.

Collaborators:

With thanks to Dr David Boyle.

Responsibilities:

Raspberry Pi Rapid Prototyping Computer Vision Python Research & Development


Completed at:

Imperial College London

Timeframe:

Oct ‘21 - May ‘22

Pocket Kitchen is a system that helps you keep track of your food inventory, monitor expiration dates, and plan recipes before items expire.

It works by retrofitting a small device to your refrigerator or other storage unit that records items as they are added and removed.

Pocket Kitchen detects objects using a TensorFlow model trained on a tailor-made dataset, which is running on a Raspberry Pi.

The data is collected in an easy-to-use web app that can also recognise some signs of food decaying.

With Pocket Kitchen, you can reduce overbuying and make sure nothing goes to waste! Read my Master's thesis to learn more.
