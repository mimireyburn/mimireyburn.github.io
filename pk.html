POCKET KITCHEN

Tackling the UK's growing household food waste through automation with machine vision.

With thanks to Dr David Boyle.

Machine Vision Raspberry Pi Rapid Prototyping

Oct 2021 - Jun 2022

How can automation strengthen behaviours that reduce food waste at home? WRAP discovered that the behaviours adopted during Covid lockdowns, such as taking a food inventory, managing expiry dates, and cooking new recipes based on available ingredients, led to a 43% reduction in UK household food waste. As we return to our busy lives post-Covid, can we find ways to sustain these behaviours? Pocket Kitchen offers an affordable and sustainable solution.

Pocket Kitchen is an intelligent device that connects your fridge or cupboard to an IoT system. It automatically records food you add or remove, tracks expiry dates, and suggests recipes to utilise expiring ingredients. It also introduces a unique food decay recognition system, using a custom fresh food dataset with metadata on freshness.

Key features:

  • End-to-end system for food inventory powered by machine vision.
  • Integrated decay state recognition.
  • Custom open-source dataset for integrated delay recognition
